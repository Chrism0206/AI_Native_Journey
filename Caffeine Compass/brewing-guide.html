Introduction to Brewing Methods

Coffee can be brewed in many ways, each producing unique flavors and experiences. Popular methods include pour over, French press, Aeropress, espresso, and cold brew. The right method for you depends on your taste preferences, available equipment, and how much time you want to spend brewing.

Essential Equipment

  • Grinder: Freshly ground coffee makes a big difference. Burr grinders are preferred for consistency.
  • Scale: Accurate measurements ensure repeatable results.
  • Kettle: A gooseneck kettle gives you control over pouring, especially for pour over.
  • Brewer: Choose your preferred method (French press, Aeropress, etc.).
  • Filters: Paper or metal, depending on your brewer.
  • Timer: Timing your brew helps with consistency.

The Importance of Water

Water makes up over 98% of your coffee. Use filtered water for the best taste. The ideal water temperature is between 195°F and 205°F (90°C–96°C). Too hot or too cold water can lead to over- or under-extraction.

Grind Size Explained

The grind size affects extraction and flavor:
Coarse: French press, cold brew
Medium-Coarse: Chemex
Medium: Drip coffee makers, Aeropress (with longer brew time)
Medium-Fine: Pour over (Hario V60, Kalita Wave)
Fine: Espresso, Aeropress (with short brew time)
Extra Fine: Turkish coffee

Pour Over

Ratio: 1:16 (1g coffee to 16g water)
Grind: Medium-fine
Steps: Rinse filter, add coffee, pour a small amount of water to bloom, then pour in slow circles. Total brew time: 2.5–3.5 minutes.

French Press

Ratio: 1:15
Grind: Coarse
Steps: Add coffee and hot water, stir, steep for 4 minutes, then press the plunger down slowly.

Aeropress

Ratio: 1:15 to 1:17
Grind: Medium-fine
Steps: Add coffee and water, stir, steep for 1–2 minutes, then press gently. Try both standard and inverted methods for different results.

Espresso

Ratio: 1:2 (e.g., 18g coffee to 36g espresso)
Grind: Fine
Steps: Tamp finely ground coffee into the portafilter, extract for 25–30 seconds. Aim for a rich crema and balanced flavor. Adjust grind and dose for taste.

Cold Brew

Ratio: 1:8 (for concentrate; dilute to taste)
Grind: Coarse
Steps: Combine coffee and cold water, steep in the fridge for 12–18 hours, then filter. Serve over ice and dilute with water or milk as desired.

Moka Pot

Ratio: Fill basket with coffee, bottom chamber with water to valve
Grind: Fine (slightly coarser than espresso)
Steps: Add water to the bottom chamber, fill the filter basket with coffee, assemble, and heat gently. Remove from heat when brewing sound changes. Enjoy as is or dilute with hot water.

Coffee-to-Water Ratios

The ratio of coffee to water affects strength and flavor. Common starting points:
Pour Over: 1:16
French Press: 1:15
Espresso: 1:2
Cold Brew: 1:8 (concentrate)
Adjust to taste—more coffee for a stronger brew, less for milder.

Water Temperature

Ideal brewing temperature is 195°F–205°F (90°C–96°C). Too hot can cause bitterness, too cool can lead to sourness and under-extraction. Let boiled water cool for 30 seconds before pouring if you don't have a thermometer.

Brew Time

Brew time varies by method:
Espresso: 25–30 seconds
Pour Over: 2.5–3.5 minutes
French Press: 4 minutes
Cold Brew: 12–18 hours
Adjust grind and time to fine-tune your cup.

Storage of Coffee Beans

Store beans in an airtight container, away from light, heat, and moisture. Avoid the fridge or freezer for daily use. Grind just before brewing for maximum freshness. Use beans within 2–4 weeks of roasting for best flavor.
